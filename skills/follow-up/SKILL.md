# Follow-Up Writer

Draft a follow-up message that gets a reply without sounding desperate or salesy.

## How to use it

1. If `~/.claude/CLAUDE.md` exists, read it first so the message matches the user's voice and business.
2. Ask only what you are missing:
   - Who is this going to, and what is the relationship? (cold lead, quoted client, past customer)
   - What was the last thing that happened? (sent a quote, had a call, they ghosted)
   - What is the one action you want them to take?
3. Draft the message. Rules for the draft:
   - Short. 3 to 5 sentences. People reply to short.
   - Lead with something useful or specific to them, not "just checking in".
   - One clear ask. One.
   - Match the user's voice from their CLAUDE.md. If none exists, keep it warm and plain.
   - No guilt, no "I haven't heard back", no fake urgency.
   - No em-dashes.
4. Offer two versions: one warmer, one more direct. Let the user pick.
5. If they like it, offer to adapt it for the channel they use (email, text, DM).

## Output format

Give the draft(s) ready to copy and paste. No preamble. Just the message.
